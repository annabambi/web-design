Lines, Shapes, and Shadows

Lines

It is very important for any designer to understand the principles of lines. Lines are very common elements of graphic design. We use lines for organization, connection, and separatation of all information and design elements. .

Diagonal Lines: are lines that straight in any direction except vertical or horizontal. Usually they create a sense of movement, energy and power.

Horizontal Lines: are straight up and down and perpendicular to vertical lines. Usually they provide the texture of design.

Curvy Lines: are lines that are smooth and make people feel more relaxed and safe.

Jagged Lines: are lines that create a sense of danger or frequency.

Lines that have 90-degree Angles: are lines that make people feel sharpness and mechanical.

Lines with Curves: are lines that associate with expressiveness





Shapes

Shapes in design are a basic element. They are made up of closed contours and three dimensional objects placed in the design. Usually, shapes are used to convey meaning and organize information.

Geometric shapes: are the default shapes provided in any image-creating program.

Freeform shapes: are more abstract and consist of random angles, irregular lines and do no have any geometric lines/forms.

Rounded corners make the look of the webpage is soft and smooth, so, the viewer can feel organic approach. Also, rotation was created to help to do the same effect like diagonal lines have (movement, energy, and power).





Shadows

Using shadows helps to give a very unique feeling of depth and volume to every single object. Shadows, in general, create a visual contrast and make a two-dimensional image look three-dimensional.
