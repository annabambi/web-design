Color Theory

Colors

It is very important for any designer to understand the principles of colors. Every color has its unique meaning. By using different tones of colors you can give to the viewer any information you want. Every single color should be chosen very carefully because the colors have a huge psychological power.

Red Color: Red is rich and aggressive color. It brings energy and high blood pressure. Usually this color is used in sports and also can symbolize love and passion.

Orange Color: Orange is also energetic and aggressive color but less than red. Orange symbolizes happiness, enthusiasm and, also, human appetize and healt. Usually this color is used for hunting equipment and safe jackets.

Yellow Color: Yellow is color of sunshine and smiles. It brings people warm feelings and good mood. At the same time, it is most visible color on the spectrum, that catches people’s eyes. Usually this color is used for safety equipment, taxi, school busses, safety lines at the metros.

Green Color: Green is peaceful color of nature. It symbolizes wealthy, stability, health, organic products and also finance side. Usually this color is used to appeal visitors’ sense of nature.

Blue Color: Blue is color of openness, calming, faith, cleanness and intelengess. This color helps to calm down the feelings. Sometimes more darker tones of blue can be a cause of sadness. This color mostly is used for logos of different big companies and it is one of the most favourites colors of universities.

Purple Color: Purple is color of luxury, wealth and extravagance. Usually this color is used for symbolization of wine and flowers. Purple color is one of the least-used colors in web design.

White Color: White is color of cleanness, purity and freshness. This color represents innocence and new step in life. White color is used for weddings, background for web pages and developing simplicity.

Black Color: Black is color of strength, elegance and power, but often it can symbolize fairs, mystery and death. This color represents different kinds of view, , depending on how it’s used.

Color temperature

The temperature of the image depends on the colors. By increasing the orange levels the image will become warmer and happier. And, otherwise, by reducing the temperature of the image it becomes colder and less inviting.



Primary, Secondary, and Tertiary colors

Primary colors: Primary colors are the wheel of red, yellow, and blue. Those colors form a triangle on the color wheel.

Secondary colors: If to mix two primary colors we get secondary colors (orange, green, and purple).

Tertiary colors: If to mix secondary colors you get tertiary colors: vermilion (red-orange), marigold (yellow-orange), chartreuse (yellow-green), aquamarine (blue-green), violet (blue-purple), and magenta (red-purple).



RGB and CMYK

RGB colors: The RGB color are used on the web. The RGB color system defines all colors as combination of three different colors: shades of red, green, and blue.

CMYK color: The CMYK color model is used in color printing and also is used to describe the printing color itself. The colors in that system are cyan, magenta, yellow and key (black).
